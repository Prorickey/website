Over 50% of US rivers and streams fail to meet environmental standards, partly because monitoring relies on stationary sensors that leave large portions of water unaccounted for. To address this, my team and I built NEMO — an IoT-enabled autonomous submarine that collects temperature, turbidity, and pH data and streams it to a live web dashboard with 3D/2D globe visualization. The hardware runs on an ESP-32 with modular sensors housed in a 3D-printed, fit-sealed enclosure. I was responsible for CAD modeling the submarine body and soldering the electronics. We plan to add steering fins, a depth pump, and fully calibrated sensors in a future version.
